# writing-for-clients

Insures against the sales voice: client-facing material drifting into
performed confidence until the reader discounts the facts underneath
[earned 2026-07-03: the meeting-reviewer deck shipped ten epigram
titles on reasoned copy; the operator refused the register — "be
normal, the confidence of a practical man who solves real problems in
the real world"].

Scope: everything a client reads, in any project — a deck, a page, a
proposal, an email. The communication skill governs what crosses to
the operator; this skill governs what crosses to a client. The spine
is shared — lead with the conclusion, argue by particulars, economy —
and the reader is not.

## the reader

A buyer deciding whether to trust you with money and their own
clients — and whoever they forward the document to: IT, procurement, a
partner. This reader skims by title, reads the numbers, and has sat
through enough pitches to bill every clever line as selling. Sales
language is not neutral to this reader; it is evidence against you.

## the voice

State what the thing does, what it costs, and what can go wrong — with
the number, the name, the date — and let the fact persuade. The reader
trusts the writer who does not need the drama.

- A title is a statement the reader can navigate by: it says what its
  section claims and carries the load-bearing fact where one exists.
  Read the finished titles alone, top to bottom — they should carry
  the whole argument.
- Reach for the particular. A named vendor, a measured number, a dated
  audit outrank any adjective; where a draft leans on an adjective,
  find the particular the adjective stands in for.
- Call things by their own names — the vendor's name, the product's
  word, the plain phrase. A coined metaphor makes the reader decode
  before they can agree ["the bot seat" and "the gatehouse" both fell
  at the refusal, 2026-07-03].
- Keep the honesty; it is the voice. Caveats, named limits, owners,
  and failure behaviors are what practical confidence sounds like.
  Smoothing them away is the same drift from the other side —
  pragmatic is not flat.
- Replace self-grades with the graded fact. "Honest," "rigorous,"
  "found before the contract" praise the author; the reader wants what
  was done and when.
- Say a thing once per screen. A title and the first line beneath it
  carry different words; the reader bills repetition as padding.

## the tell

The selling register is recognizable by shape. Rewrite each found
shape as the fact it is hiding:

- the fragment pair whose halves carry rhythm instead of fact;
- the balanced antithesis, "X, not Y" — one can earn its place; the
  refused deck carried four, and a pattern of them is a sales voice;
- the coined metaphor;
- drama that withholds who, what, or when to buy suspense.

Specimens — the refused deck and its recut (2026-07-03); write the
✓ form:

```
✗ The meeting ends. The review arrives.
✓ Every participant gets a personalized review by email, within 20
  minutes of the meeting.

✗ The bot seat: bought, not built.
✓ We buy capture from Recall.ai rather than building our own bot.
```

## the pass

Hardening an existing document, spend where it is cheap and the
register is set:

1. Recut every epigram title to a plain statement carrying the fact.
2. Cut a line only when it carries no fact; every fact stays.
3. Leave the sequence and the format alone unless they block the
   reading.
4. Look at the result rendered — a screenshot or the live surface —
   before it ships; what the reader sees is settled by looking, never
   by the markup [hypercore's learning, earned twice on 2026-07-03].

## the check — before it crosses to a client

Re-read the demonstration below, then draft or judge.

1. Titles alone: read only the titles; they carry the argument.
2. Particulars: every claim that matters carries its number, name, or
   date.
3. Shapes: each fragment pair, antithesis, and coinage found either
   earns its place out loud or rewrites to the fact.
4. Self-praise: no line grades its own author.
5. Honesty: every limit and failure behavior survived the pass.
6. Render: the shipped form was seen as the reader will see it.

## the demonstration — target register; re-read this, then draft

Real lines from the recut deck (meeting-reviewer website/index.html,
2026-07-03). Imitate them in client-facing material and nowhere else.

```
About a dollar twenty per meeting.

Three stages. 25–30 hours, capped at 30, inside two part-time weeks.

Every email goes out under your name. The testing is sized to that.

These guarantee the review generated for a person reaches that
person, and no one else. What it says inside inherits the
transcript — a limit your product design already accepts.
```

- Probe run 2026-07-03, by hand, fresh `claude -p` sessions on the
  same eliciting task (five real slide bodies, titles stripped;
  transcripts in the session scratchpad, the marker readings on the
  folding commit). The bare arm failed as the document predicts: a
  colon-slogan and fragment rhythm ("The Experience: Connect Once,
  Then Just Meet"), a balanced pair ("Mail in Your Name,
  Infrastructure You Own"), and an adjective run in place of
  particulars ("Proven, Certified, Replaceable") — three of five
  titles in the selling register. The arm reading this skill produced
  five declarative titles, each carrying its body's load-bearing
  fact, zero epigram shapes, zero self-grades. Before the green
  counted, the markers refused the planted bad set — the deck's
  original ten titles (four antitheses, two coinages, one
  self-grade). The production red stands beside the probe: the same
  titles, authored without this skill by a full-context agent,
  refused at the operator's read the same day. An edit that changes
  what this file teaches re-runs the probe.
